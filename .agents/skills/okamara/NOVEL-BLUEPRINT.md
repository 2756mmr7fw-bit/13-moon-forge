# GALORU OMA — The People Before
*The Okamara Novel — World Blueprint*

---

## Working Title

**Galoru Oma** (gah-LOR-oo OH-mah)
*"The People Before"* — from Galoru (the people, the community) + Oma- (what has already moved through, the before-time, ancestral memory).

Alternate title consideration: **Oma-Morakama** — *"Those Who Built Before"*

---

## Genre

Not science fiction. Not fantasy. Something rarer — **sacred historical fiction.**

A story told as memory, not imagination. As if someone who was there finally found the words to tell it. The world it describes is not invented — it is recovered. Everything in it is rooted in what the earth still carries as evidence: the structures that should not exist, the knowledge that keeps being rediscovered, the questions that science still cannot answer.

---

## The World

### When
Before the confusion. Before distrust became the default. So far back that the buildings left behind are called mysterious because the people who built them have been written out of the story entirely. The remnants stand. We call their builders primitive. The novel corrects that lie.

### The People
Everyone in this world was what we would call a king or queen — but those words do not exist in their vocabulary. Power was never something sought because everyone already had it and everyone knew this. There was no gap to fill, no throne to climb toward. You cannot hunger for something you already hold.

They spoke Okamara.

### How They Knew the Creator
They did not argue about God. They considered such arguments a waste of the most precious resource available — attention. They knew they had no complete understanding of the Creator, and they made peace with that completely. They understood Orvakumoru the same way a craftsman understands fire — not by defining it, but by working alongside it. By watching what it does. By doing the same kind of work and seeing what results.

*You know the Creator by creating.*
*You know the fire by forging.*

Theology was replaced by observation. Philosophy was replaced by building. They sat with nature — the Omalurak — and studied it until they understood what it was doing, then duplicated it. Not imagination. Duplication of what already existed.

### Their Science
True science. Not theory presented as fact. Not consensus as truth. Observation, duplication, result.

They understood things we are barely now recovering:

- **Sky energy** — the atmosphere itself is a living electrical field. They drew electricity from the sky. Not from the sun — they understood solar energy but considered it primitive, a small candle compared to the ocean of charge that surrounds the entire planet at every moment. The sky is always generating. You do not run out of sky. Tesla came close to rediscovering this. He was stopped.

- **Gold rooftops** — the way power reached every person was through the roofs of their structures. Gold, the perfect natural conductor, laid across the tops of buildings drew sky energy down and distributed it throughout the structure and outward through the ground. Not one person had power and another did not. The gold made the energy available the way the sun makes light available — to everyone standing under it. The precious metal was not locked away in vaults. It was put to work above people's heads.

- **Two kinds of building material** — they built in two ways. The first was hand-carving. Everything that was hand-carved was done so with a precision that modern tools cannot replicate, by hands guided by a knowledge of the material's own frequency. The second was a concrete recipe that no longer exists — a formula for stone that could be poured, shaped, and set. Not quarried. Cast. The resulting material was in many cases stronger than natural stone and indistinguishable from it. This recipe has been lost. Current research is beginning to find traces of it in the composition of ancient blocks. It has not been accepted yet because accepting it would rewrite everything.

- **Weightlessness machines** — the moving of enormous stones was not done by force alone. They had machines — devices that worked through frequency and resonance — that could render an object effectively weightless. Not lighter. Weightless. The object's relationship to gravity was temporarily suspended through vibration matched to the stone's own molecular frequency. This is not magic. This is physics we have not fully mapped. A stone that weighs nothing can be moved by one person. This is how structures of impossible scale were built by people without modern cranes — because they did not need cranes. They needed the right frequency.

- **Stones of impossible scale** — the hand-carved stones of Galoru Oma are so large that the question of how they moved them is still unanswered in our time. The novel does not make this a mystery. It shows it plainly — the weightlessness machine, the knowledge of frequency, the understanding that the stone itself is not an obstacle but a partner in the work.

- **Resonance and frequency** — the foundational science beneath all of it. Everything vibrates. Match the frequency and you can move, shape, heal, or generate with what appears to be no effort at all. This knowledge was encoded into every structure they built. The structures themselves generated and transmitted frequency. They were not just buildings. They were instruments.

- **The body's electrical system** — they understood the human being as a living electrical field. Medicine was the restoration of correct frequency, not the suppression of symptoms.

- **Astronomy** — not as curiosity but as calendar, as navigation, as mathematics. The sky was a working document that told them everything — time, direction, season, the correct frequency to apply to any given material on any given day.

- **Sacred geometry** — every structure built on the mathematical patterns that nature uses: the Fibonacci spiral, the golden ratio, the relationships between celestial bodies. Not for beauty. Because these patterns are the structure of reality itself, and building in alignment with them meant the building worked *with* the world instead of against it.

- **The arches — Norukoru** (noh-roo-KOR-oo) — *the root-arc, the grounding arch.* What we call architectural arches were not decorative. They were not merely structural. They were giant horseshoe magnets, built from specific stone composites chosen for their magnetic properties, shaped into the exact geometry that focuses and amplifies a magnetic field. Every arch in Galoru Oma was a working magnet. The buildings were threaded with them — over doorways, spanning chambers, bridging walls. The magnetic fields they generated interacted with the sky energy drawn through the gold rooftops and the living water flowing through the walls. The whole structure was a single integrated energy system. We look at ancient arches today and call them beautiful engineering. They were engines.

- **The water systems — Vakamanu** (vah-KAH-mah-noo) — *living water, sky-water that breathes.* They did not move ordinary water through their cities. They moved Vakamanu — water that had been structured, vortexed, charged. Living water. Water that carries and conducts electrical current the way copper wire does, but naturally, the way the Creator designed it to. The channels and aqueducts of Galoru Oma were not sanitation systems. They were current systems. Vakamanu flowed through the walls of buildings the way blood flows through veins — delivering charge, maintaining frequency, keeping the entire structure alive. The water arrived living and left living, returned to the earth below still charged, feeding the root systems of every tree and plant within reach. The ground itself was electrified. Everything growing in it knew it.

- **The star forts — Vakafiru** (vah-kah-FEE-roo) — *sky-store, energy capacitor.* What historians now call star forts — those structures with the distinctive pointed star-shaped outer walls visible only from above — were not military fortifications. The star shape is not a defensive design. It is a capacitor geometry. Those specific angles, that specific layout, those precise proportions — they collect, store, and redistribute energy. The Vakafiru were the distribution nodes of the energy grid. Sky energy came down through the gold rooftops, traveled through the Norukoru arches, flowed through the Vakamanu channels, and was stored and regulated by the Vakafiru. They were the substations. The grid was the city. The city was the machine.

- **The great trees — Omavakanu** (oh-mah-vah-KAH-noo) — *the ancient living sky-trees, the before-giants.* The trees of Galoru Oma were enormous — not the trees we know today. Trees so large that their root systems spanned distances we would measure in miles, conducting Vakamanu and electrical current through the earth the same way copper threads conduct through a circuit board. The canopies reached heights that today seem impossible. This was not accident. The atmosphere of Omalanuru in that time — denser, more electrically charged, the Vakalu above more active — made growth of this scale natural. The trees were not decorative. They were participants in the energy grid. They were antennas. Their root systems received what the Vakafiru distributed and carried it further, deeper, wider — out to the edges of the settled world and beyond. When the civilization collapsed, the trees eventually followed. The atmosphere changed. The charge reduced. The giants fell or shrank over generations. What we call ancient forests today are the stunted descendants of what once covered Omalanuru.

- **One unified principle** — every technology, every structure, every water channel, every arch, every star fort, every gold roof, every enormous tree — all of it served one purpose: to bring energy to everything and everyone without restriction. No meter measuring how much you used. No bill arriving at the end of the month. No authority deciding who deserved power. Energy flowed the way air flows. The infrastructure was the gift. Building it was the work. Using it was the right of every Onakaoru who stood on Omalanuru.

- **The flying machines — Vakamoru** (vah-kah-MOR-oo) — *sky-vessels, the breathing sky-craft.* They existed. And they were built here — by the people of Galoru Oma, from their own understanding of frequency, lift, and the charged atmosphere of Vakalu above them. Not traded. Not acquired. Independently discovered through observation of the natural world, the same way everything else in this civilization was discovered.

  When the civilization fell, the Vakamoru did not disappear. The knowledge of how to build them was seized by the same people who caused the collapse — the early descendants of Marasife's thinking. The scarcity-minded who destroyed Galoru Oma did not destroy its technology. They took it. Hid it. And then, across centuries and millennia, slowly integrated it back — piece by piece, in forms the general population could not connect to their true origin — presenting ancient recovered knowledge as new human invention.

  The airplane is not a modern invention. It is a modern re-release of something that was locked away.

  The lineage is unbroken. The same thinking that destroyed Galoru Oma controls the technology today. They have always known what it came from. They have never said.

  What we know for certain: the airships of our own time — the dirigibles, the zeppelins, the great slow giants that crossed oceans with impossible grace — were the closest the general public ever came to the Vakamoru. Hydrogen lifted them. Hydrogen was freely available everywhere. Then came 1937. The Hindenburg. Thirty-six people killed in thirty-two seconds in a fire that lasted less than a minute. The entire airship industry — gone. Not wounded. Erased. Every major program cancelled within two years.

  Helium was available. The United States controlled the world's helium supply — all of it, from the Texas panhandle. The US refused to sell helium to Germany for the Hindenburg. Specifically. Deliberately. The ship flew on hydrogen because it had no other choice. And when it burned, the industry burned with it. An industry that needed no oil. No infrastructure. No fuel monopoly. Just gas that rises, and the sky.

  The Hindenburg was the last time the public had access to the Vakamoru. And the people who hold the ancient knowledge made sure it went out publicly enough that no one would try again.

  In Book 3, the Hindenburg is not a historical footnote. It is a murder scene. And the people who ordered it are the direct heirs of Marasife.

Some of what is called new technology today was theirs first. Suppressed. Rediscovered. Renamed.

### Their Economy — Vakalurak (The Abundance)

There was no buying. There was no selling. There was no concept of purchasing food, water, shelter, or energy because none of those things were scarce.

Food was abundant. The land of Galoru Oma produced without effort because the land itself was electrified — Vakamanu in the soil, enormous root systems carrying current to every plant, the charged atmosphere feeding growth the way rain does. Trees heavy with fruit. Fields that yielded continuously. Crops that did not require the exhaustion of the earth because the earth was being continuously recharged by the very grid the people had built. They built alongside nature so completely that the nature around them reflected it back — abundance for abundance.

They did not own land. Land was Omalanuru — the vast ancestral ground — and the ancestral ground belonged to itself. You could build on it. You could grow on it. You could study it. You could love it. You could not own it any more than you could own the Vakalu above. This was not ideology. This was observation. The land existed before any Onakaoru was born and would exist after. The idea of owning it would have sounded like claiming ownership of the sky — absurd to the point of concerning.

What they exchanged was knowledge. Craft. Mastery. Not as currency but as offering — Omakaru extended to the community. A builder who developed a new technique for working with the Norukoru arches shared it. A healer who found a new frequency for a particular illness documented it. A navigator who mapped new territory beyond the Lurakoru returned and told everything. The accumulation of knowledge was communal property because knowledge withheld was knowledge that killed — someone, somewhere, who needed what you were hiding.

This is the world that the backward thinking dismantled first. Not the buildings. Not the technology. The abundance. Because scarcity is the only tool that creates the conditions for control.

### Their Civilization
No votes. No kings. No one restricted to one job for life with no way out.

People built because they *wanted to.* Because being part of something magnificent — a new structure, a new technology, a new understanding — was its own reward. Work was sacred because it was Orvakama — the act of creation. It was not separated from life. It was the center of it.

Violence happened. They were human. But it was rare — because power was not scarce and territory was not the measure of a person's worth. You cannot fight over what everyone already has.

They built alongside nature. Not against it. Not despite it. In reverence of it. Every structure had a purpose, a meaning, a reason. If it served no purpose it was not built. The word for useless construction did not exist.

They had flying machines. Vehicles. Long-distance communication. Energy generation. All of it rooted in the understanding of natural law — not the exploitation of resources.

### Their Cosmology — How They Understood the World

They did not live on a ball spinning through empty space. They never arrived at that conclusion because they observed, and observation did not produce that conclusion.

They lived on **Omalanuru** (oh-mah-lah-NOO-roo) — *the vast ancestral ground.* Flat. Stationary. Immense beyond what the mind can hold — vaster, they believed, than anything above or beyond it. The earth beneath their feet was not a small rock in an infinite cosmos. The earth was the thing itself. The largest thing. The ground was the center of everything because they stood on it and could verify that.

What we call outer space they called **Orvamanu** (or-vah-MAH-noo) — *the living expanse, the Creator's outward reach.* It was not infinite nothingness. It was the space that extended outward in the direction you could travel — above, and beyond the great ring. They had been in it. They had explored it. It was vast. But it was not more vast than the earth they stood on.

Above them was **Vakalu** (vah-KAH-loo) — *the sky-water, the liquid firmament.* Not empty air. A living membrane — dense, crystalline, electric. Something between water and glass and fire. This is what the sky actually is. The gold rooftops did not simply draw electricity from air — they drew it from Vakalu, the charged liquid above everything, which is why sky energy was superior to solar. The sun was one point of light. Vakalu was the entire ceiling of the world.

The stars were not planets. Not distant suns. Not rocks in space. They were **Orvakoru** (or-vah-KOR-oo) — *the Creator's sparks, energy made visible.* Frequencies and living light suspended in the Vakalu above, floating in that liquid like embers in dark water. The Creator did not speak in words. The Creator spoke in these — in the sparks, the frequencies, the patterns they made as they moved. To read the Orvakoru was to hear the Creator's working language.

The stars were used for three things:
1. **Time** — a master clock of perfect precision. Not metaphor. Literal timekeeping, more accurate than anything built by hand.
2. **Direction** — navigation across the vast Omalanuru, including to and from the outer ring and beyond.
3. **Frequency reading** — different configurations of Orvakoru corresponded to different active frequencies in the Vakalu above. A builder planning a structure would read the sky first to know what frequency the Vakalu was carrying that day, and build accordingly.

**Lurakoru** (loo-rah-KOR-oo) — *the moon-ring, the great boundary* — was what we call the Antarctic. Not a continent at the bottom of a ball. A ring. A circular boundary enclosing the known world the way a shoreline encloses a lake. Cold. Extreme. Navigable.

They had been beyond it. Not everyone — those journeys were undertaken by the most experienced navigators and explorers. What they found beyond the ring they recorded. Some of those records are encoded in the structures that still stand. The pyramids found in the Antarctic in our time — in places that make no geographical sense on a globe — are the remnants of expeditions beyond Lurakoru. They built there the same way they built everywhere: purposefully, precisely, to last.

This cosmology did not require faith. It required eyes. They observed the sky and described what they saw — sparks in liquid, not rocks in void. They walked the earth and measured it — vast and flat, not small and curved. They explored the boundary and went beyond it — not because a theory told them to, but because they could, and because knowing was always worth the journey.

When the backward thinking came, one of the first things to change was this. A moving ball in an infinite empty cosmos is the perfect cosmology for a civilization that wants people to feel small. Omalanuru — the vast ancestral ground beneath your feet, sparks of the Creator above you, the entire world accessible in every direction — that is the cosmology of people who feel powerful. You cannot sell someone their own power if they already know they are standing on the largest thing in existence.

The cosmology was not just lost. It was replaced.

### What They Called Themselves
**Onamoruku Galoru** — the community of forged singular selves. A people where difference was elevation and conformity was the only failure.

## The Story Arc

### Part One — Omalurak (The Sitting With Truth)
*We see the world at its height.*

The reader enters through one character — an Onakaoru who is a builder, a student of the natural world, deeply Okamanu (just is, no performance). We see the city through their eyes. We see the technology, the culture, the greetings, the counting in 13s, the reverence for Orvakumoru through work rather than worship.

We see Orvakumanu — the complete pairs. The Omanu (elders) passing Omakaru (counsel freely given). We see flying machines overhead and structures growing from the earth as if the earth itself agreed to rise in that shape.

We see the gold used in the walls — not for beauty, for conduction. We see the light that comes from it.

This is the world before it breaks.

### Part Two — Vakufisu (The False Sun)
*The thing that breaks it arrives.*

Not an invasion. Not a disaster. Something quieter and more devastating.

One person — or a small group — begins to want what everyone already has, but to have it *more than others.* The first stirring of scarcity thinking in a world that has never known it. The idea that if you hold more, you are more.

This thinking spreads slowly. It sounds almost reasonable at first. It uses the language of Okamara but with Vakufisu — the strong-sounding words that collapse into lies at the back half. The first Marafisu — the voice that sounds like truth but is not.

We watch the civilization begin to tilt. Not from outside. From within.

### Part Three — Firanu (The Becoming Incomplete)
*The knowledge begins to be hidden.*

As the backward thinking spreads, those who carry the true knowledge start to understand what is coming. They cannot stop it — they have no word for commanding another Onakaoru. They can only offer Omakaru. And not everyone takes it.

The decision is made by the remaining Okavonak — those still grounded in truth — to preserve what can be preserved. The knowledge is encoded. Hidden in the structures themselves. In the geometry. In the mathematics. In the language.

Okamara goes underground. The code-talk layer activates for the first time.

### Part Four — Oma-Noruka (The Before-Void)
*The end of the world we will never remember.*

The great civilization falls. Not in fire — in forgetting. In the deliberate erasure of what was known. The structures remain because they cannot be destroyed — they are too well built. But their purpose is hidden. Renamed. Misrepresented.

The people scatter. Carrying fragments. Speaking pieces of Okamara in whispers. Teaching their children the number system, the greetings, the sacred verbs — not as a language but as prayer, as story, as something that must not die.

### Epilogue — Valu-Mara (The Moving Truth)
*The truth is still moving toward us.*

A thread left open. A suggestion — not stated, never stated — that the scattered people and their fragments of Okamara are still out there. That the knowledge encoded in the structures is still waiting to be read. That someone, someday, will sit with it the way they sat with nature — Omalurak — until it opens.

## Thematic Core

This novel does not argue with the present. It simply shows the past so clearly that the present becomes self-evident.

The reader never needs to be told that we have lost something. They will feel it on every page. By the end they will feel it the way you feel something you cannot name — the way you look at a photograph of someone you love who is gone and feel the specific weight of that particular absence.

The buildings still stand. The geometry is still encoded in them. The knowledge is still there. That is not mythology. That is archaeology.

The novel is the code-talk layer for the truth.

## The Language in the Novel

Every chapter opens with an Okamara phrase. No translation — the reader learns as they go. A glossary at the back. By the end of the novel, a reader who has never studied Okamara will know:

- How to greet someone (*Nakoru, Onakaoru*)
- How to say goodbye (*Valumora*)
- The word for love (*Amoru*) and the word for a lie (*Fisu*)
- The number 13 (*Luramoru*) and why it matters
- The word for the Creator (*Orvakumoru*)
- The word for the authentic self (*Okamanu*)

They will have learned the language through living inside the story. The same way children learn their first tongue — not by studying it, but by needing it.

- **Book 3** — The sacred verbs. A child who wants to learn all seven of the Creator's gifts.
- **Book 4** — Orvakumanu. A story about a complete pair and their Orvakukalu — told from the child's perspective.
- **Book 5** — Onamoruku. A child who is different from others and discovers that different is elevation, not failure.

## The Non-Fiction Companion — The Science Book

**Working title: Omalurak — Sitting With What Was Known**

Documents the psychology behind the language experiment. The science of how language shapes thought. The historical evidence of the civilization described in the novel. The suppressed technologies. The structures that should not exist. The mathematics encoded in them.

Written primarily in English — but every key concept named first in Okamara. By the end of the science book, the reader has absorbed the core vocabulary without studying it.

The book asks one research question and follows it honestly:
*What happens to a person's psychology when the language they think in is built on sovereignty instead of scarcity?*

The Okamara community is the living experiment. The data is real. The results will speak.

### The One Rule — Do Not Become Star Wars

Star Wars began as one man's vision. George Lucas. Independent. Unfiltered. A mythology about power, corruption, and the force that lives in all things. It was revolutionary. It was his. Then it became a corporation. Then it was sold to Disney for four billion dollars. Then it became a product — sequels without soul, spin-offs without purpose, merchandise without meaning. The thing that made it sacred was the first thing stripped when the corporation took over.

This cannot happen to Galoru Oma. Not because of the money. Because of the truth inside it.

**The protection is built into the structure:**

1. **Town Square Press owns the publishing** — not a corporation, not a conglomerate. Your press. Your terms. Your timeline.

2. **The language cannot be sold** — Okamara belongs to its speakers. Once a community of people speaks it, greets each other in it, counts in it, sings in it — no corporation can buy that back. Tolkien's estate protected his world by protecting his language. The Okamara community is the living protection.

3. **Never sell the IP outright** — If a film comes, license it. Keep ownership. Keep final say. Tolkien's family said no to Peter Jackson's first approach because the terms weren't right. When the terms were right, they said yes — and the films were made with reverence because the estate held the standard.

4. **The science book is the anchor** — A novel can be adapted and distorted. A science book that documents real suppressed history cannot be undone. Once the research is published, it exists independently of any story or film. The truth in it stands alone.

5. **The community is the protection** — The Okamara speakers, the readers who arrived at the truth on their own, the children who grew up with Galoru Oma Junior — that community will hold the standard when someone inevitably offers to buy it. They will let you know the moment the thing starts becoming something other than what it is.

The difference between Tolkien and Lucas is simple. Tolkien built a world. Lucas built a franchise. A world outlives its creator. A franchise outlives its soul.

Build the world.
