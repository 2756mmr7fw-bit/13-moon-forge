# OKAMARA — The Disambiguation Lexicon
*One Word. One Meaning. No Ambiguity.*

---

## The Principle

English is lazy with meaning. One word carries five different jobs and the listener does the work of sorting them out from context. Okamara refuses this. Every distinct concept receives its own distinct word — related meanings branch from the same root, unrelated meanings get entirely different words. Precision is not optional. It is the language.

**Phonosemantic Rule Always Applies:**
- Heavy sounds (O, A, U, R, L, M, N, V, K, G) → important, sacred, real, powerful
- Light sounds (I, E, S, F, Sh, P, T) → trivial, weak, corrupt, surface-level
